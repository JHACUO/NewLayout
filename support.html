1.8 Support Services (academic counselling, LIL, ASU)

Studying is rewarding and worthwhile, but sometimes you may need help. ACU Onlineâ€™s student services can help you navigate your way through some of the more challenging parts of uni life. There is a range of services available to you at ACU Online. Please contact them for support as you progress through the unit.

If you are experiencing difficulties with learning, life issues or pastoral/spiritual concerns, or have a disability/medical condition which may impact on your studies, you are advised to notify your Online Facilitator (OF), Lecturer in Charge (LiC) and/or one of the services listed below as soon as possible.

Counselling services

Speak confidentially and freely to ACU Online's counsellors. They will listen to your circumstances and work with you to explore possible ways forward. Visit the Counselling page in the Student Portal to book an appointment with a counsellor, ask a counsellor a question or access the out-of-hours mental health crisis line.

Academic Integrity Module (AIM)

Academic integrity is the responsibility of all students and staff at ACU Online. All ACU Online students need to complete the Academic Integrity Module (AIM) in their first study period. You will be given access to the AIM in Canvas on enrolment. Your final results will not be released until you complete it. If you have any concerns about academic integrity, you can contact your LiC or Online Facilitator. Alternatively, the Academic Integrity team can be contacted at academic.integrity@acu.edu.au

Academic Skills Unit

As an ACU Online student, you have access to the Academic Skills Unit. The unit offers you current skills development assistance and opportunities in the areas of Academic Language and Learning, and Maths and Numeracy. You can attend workshops, drop-in sessions, ask an advisor online or make an appointment for an individual consultation. See the Launch Into Learning unit for more information. You can also self-enrol in the Academic Skills Unit.

You can also use the ASU appointment system (ACU, n.d.) to book a 30-minute consultation with an Academic Skills Advisor. A booking confirmation will be sent to your ACU Online email address. If you have any difficulties booking an appointment, contact us by email at academicskills@acu.edu.au.

Student Support Unit

A separate unit for student support has been created and you have been enrolled in it automatically. It contains key information, resources and contacts that you may need as you complete this unit and others in your course. You can view the Student Support module in Canvas.

Getting help: ASK ACUO

If you have any questions about the technical requirements, your hardware while studying with ACU Online, or anything related to getting you started and set up on your studying journey, we have a dedicated team available to help you.

The ASK ACUO team are your first port of call for all of your general enquiries via phone call on 13 A-C-U-O (13 2286) or via an online request on the AskACU Online page of ServiceNow.

Disability Support

This team can arrange reasonable adjustments to aspects of your study for temporary or ongoing mental and physical conditions that could impact your academic performance. Please contact them as early as possible after beginning this unit.

Visit the Disability support (ACU, n.d.) page in the Student Portal to register for support or book an appointment with an advisor.

First Peoples and Equity Pathways Directorate for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students

Every campus provides Information and support for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students.

Campus Ministry

Grounded in the University's Catholic identity, the Campus Ministry's outreach embraces people of all faith traditions. They are dedicated to the wellbeing of staff and students and are here for you if ou need.

The Campus Ministry team are on hand, online and at each campus. Visit the Campus Ministry website (ACU, n.d.) to learn more about online events you can attend or contact the Ministry directly.
