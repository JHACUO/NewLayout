Electronic submission, marking and return

Unless otherwise stated, your assessment will be submitted through Turnitin. Turnitin is an originality-checking and plagiarism-prevention service used for you to check your writing for citation mistakes or inappropriate copying. Turnitin accepts:

  • Text, MS Word, MS PowerPoint.

  • Postscript, PDF, RTF, HTML.

  • WordPerfect and OpenOffice.

Files must:

  • be less than 500MB

  • contain at least 20 words of text.

Turnitin is integrated with your unit in Canvas and you can access this through the Assessment section in Canvas or the actual assessment task.

For the specific details for the submission requirements on your assessment tasks, see the Submission details section of the individual assessment on the Assessment page.

Academic integrity and misconduct policy at ACU

The Academic Integrity and Misconduct Policy sets out the University's principles and commitment to academic integrity and provides frameworks for both the promotion of academic integrity among students and staff and dealing with instances of alleged academic misconduct.

Information about the procedures and processes for handling these items is available on the Academic Misconduct Procedures page.

The Academic Integrity team is responsible for analysing and reporting misconduct, and allegations of academic misconduct will be investigated by an Academic Integrity Officer. In the case of a serious allegation, a Faculty Academic Misconduct Committee will be formed.

There is more information available on the Academic Integrity and Misconduct - Policy and Procedures page.

Compulsory completion of Academic Integrity Module (AIM)

Academic integrity is the responsibility of all students and staff at ACU Online. All ACU Online students need to complete the Academic Integrity Module (AIM) in their first study period. You will be given access to the AIM in Canvas on enrolment. Your final results will not be released until you complete it. If you have any concerns about academic integrity, you can contact your LiC or Online Facilitator. Alternatively, the Academic Integrity team can be contacted at academic.integrity@acu.edu.au
